# **Topic 6.2 – Smart Cities (Domain-Specific IoT)**

This is a **very popular exam topic** because Smart Cities cover multiple IoT use-cases.
If you write this well, you score full marks easily.

---

# **1. What Are Smart Cities in IoT?**

A Smart City uses IoT technologies to improve:

* transportation
* energy usage
* public safety
* waste management
* environmental monitoring
* public services

In simple terms:

> **Smart City = IoT + Data + Automation to improve city operations and quality of life.**

---

# **2. Key Components of Smart City IoT**

Smart City IoT depends on:

### **1. Sensors**

* air quality sensors
* traffic sensors
* noise sensors
* waste level sensors
* water quality sensors

### **2. Connectivity**

Wi-Fi, LoRaWAN, 5G, Fiber networks.

### **3. Actuators**

Traffic lights, parking gates, street lights, water valves.

### **4. Cloud & Big Data**

Centralized city dashboards and analytics.

### **5. IoT Platforms**

Open IoT platforms for integrating multiple city services.

---

# **3. Major IoT Applications in Smart Cities**

This is the section examiners want.
Write at least 5–6 points for long answers.

---

## **(i) Smart Traffic Management**

* Traffic sensors track congestion
* Adaptive traffic signals adjust automatically
* Vehicle tracking improves route planning

**Benefit:** reduces traffic, fuel usage, and travel time.

---

## **(ii) Smart Parking**

* Sensors detect free parking slots
* Drivers get location via app
* Reduces time waste and traffic congestion

---

## **(iii) Smart Street Lighting**

* Lights turn ON/OFF based on movement or time
* Adjust brightness to save energy

**Actuators + sensors + timers.**

---

## **(iv) Smart Waste Management**

* Fill-level sensors in bins
* Real-time monitoring for garbage trucks
* Optimized waste collection routes

---

## **(v) Environmental Monitoring**

* Air quality sensors (PM2.5, pollution)
* Water quality sensors
* Weather monitoring

Used for health alerts and environmental policies.

---

## **(vi) Smart Energy Grids**

* Smart meters
* Automated load balancing
* Real-time energy usage monitoring

Improves power reliability and reduces wastage.

---

## **(vii) Public Safety & Security**

* IoT-enabled CCTV cameras
* Smart emergency alert systems
* Disaster monitoring (earthquake, flood sensors)

---

## **(viii) Smart Water Management**

* Leak detection sensors
* Automatic water distribution
* Real-time level monitoring in tanks/reservoirs

---

# **4. Benefits of Smart Cities**

### **1. Better Resource Management**

Energy, water, waste — all optimized.

### **2. Reduced Traffic & Pollution**

Real-time traffic control and monitoring.

### **3. Improved Public Safety**

Cameras + sensors + alert systems.

### **4. Citizen Convenience**

Apps for parking, transport, utilities.

### **5. Cost Savings**

Less manpower, less wastage, automated systems.

---

# **5. Challenges in Smart City IoT**

Keep this short for exams.

* High deployment cost
* Cybersecurity risks
* Data privacy concerns
* Device maintenance
* Integration issues between different systems
* Requires reliable network infrastructure
