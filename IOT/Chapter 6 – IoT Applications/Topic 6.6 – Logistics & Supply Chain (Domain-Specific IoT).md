# **Topic 6.6 – Logistics & Supply Chain (Domain-Specific IoT)**

This is a very important IoT application area because logistics depends heavily on tracking, monitoring, and automation — all IoT strengths.
Exams often ask:

* “Explain IoT applications in logistics.”
* “How IoT improves supply chain management?”
* “Write a short note on IoT in transportation and logistics.”

Let’s break it down clearly.

---

# **1. What Is IoT in Logistics & Supply Chain?**

IoT in logistics and supply chain refers to the use of sensors, GPS trackers, RFID, and cloud analytics to **monitor goods, vehicles, containers, warehouses, and inventory in real time**.

In simple terms:

> **IoT makes transportation, storage, and delivery faster, safer, and more efficient.**

---

# **2. Key Components Used**

### **1. RFID Tags & Scanners**

Track product movement in warehouses.

### **2. GPS Trackers**

Monitor trucks, containers, and shipments.

### **3. Environmental Sensors**

Measure temperature, humidity, vibration during transport.

### **4. Smart Warehousing Systems**

Robots, conveyor belts, automation.

### **5. Connectivity**

4G/5G, LoRa, Wi-Fi, GSM.

### **6. Cloud + Analytics**

Used for route optimization, forecasting, alerts, dashboards.

---

# **3. Major IoT Applications in Logistics & Supply Chain**

These are MUST-WRITE points.

---

## **(i) Real-Time Vehicle Tracking**

GPS-based IoT devices track:

* location
* speed
* fuel usage
* route deviations

Logistics companies use this data to optimize routes and reduce fuel consumption.

---

## **(ii) Cold Chain Monitoring**

Used for food, vaccines, and medicines.

Sensors monitor:

* temperature
* humidity
* vibration
* light exposure

Alerts are sent if conditions go out of range.

---

## **(iii) Smart Warehousing**

IoT enables:

* automated inventory checks
* robots and conveyor systems
* real-time stock updates
* smart shelves

This reduces errors and increases efficiency.

---

## **(iv) Goods Tracking During Transit**

Each package/container can be monitored using:

* RFID
* NFC
* Bluetooth beacons
* GPS sensors

You always know where a shipment is.

---

## **(v) Fleet Management**

IoT helps manage:

* fuel consumption
* engine health
* vehicle diagnostics
* driver behavior

Prevents breakdowns and reduces operating cost.

---

## **(vi) Predictive Maintenance**

IoT sensors detect early signs of failure in:

* trucks
* machines
* warehouse equipment

Maintenance is scheduled before breakdowns happen.

---

## **(vii) Automated Inventory Management**

Inventory is updated automatically using:

* RFID
* weight sensors
* barcode scanners

Reduces human effort and eliminates stock mismatch.

---

## **(viii) Route Optimization**

Cloud platforms analyze:

* weather
* traffic
* fuel consumption

Then recommend the fastest, cheapest delivery route.

---

# **4. Benefits of IoT in Logistics**

### **1. Reduced Delivery Time**

Better route planning = faster deliveries.

### **2. Lower Operational Cost**

Fuel savings, fewer breakdowns, reduced manpower.

### **3. Real-Time Visibility**

Companies know exactly where goods are at every moment.

### **4. Improved Product Quality**

Cold chain monitoring protects food and vaccines.

### **5. Fewer Losses & Theft**

Sensors detect tampering and unauthorized access.

### **6. Increased Customer Satisfaction**

Accurate tracking boosts customer trust.

---

# **5. Challenges**

Keep these short.

* High initial deployment cost
* Connectivity issues in remote areas
* Sensor calibration problems
* Data privacy concerns
* Cybersecurity threats (hacking of tracked data)
* Maintenance of tracking & sensing devices

---

* Definition

Full marks.
