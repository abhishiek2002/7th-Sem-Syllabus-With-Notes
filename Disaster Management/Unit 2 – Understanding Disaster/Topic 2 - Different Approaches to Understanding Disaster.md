# **2. Different Approaches to Understanding Disaster**

*(Importance: Very High — these approaches are repeated in theory questions, long answers, and conceptual MCQs.)*

The examiner expects you to know **how disasters are studied**, not just what they are.

Most answers should revolve around **the lens through which we analyze disasters**.

Here are the clean, high-scoring approaches:

---

# **1. *Natural Event Approach***

This is the *old-school* view.

### **What it says:**

A disaster is simply a **natural event** (earthquake, cyclone, flood) causing damage.

### **Why it’s flawed:**

It ignores human vulnerability, poor planning, bad construction, etc.
The approach is outdated but still asked in exams.

---

# **2. *Physical or Engineering Approach***

This approach looks at disasters as **failures of structures, systems, or physical safety measures**.

### Focus areas:

* building strength
* dam design
* seismic-resistant structures
* flood barriers
* infrastructure resilience

### Use:

Good for technical mitigation planning.

---

# **3. *Social Vulnerability Approach***

This is the **modern, widely accepted approach**.

### **What it says:**

Disasters happen because people are **vulnerable**, not because hazards are strong.

### Vulnerability factors include:

* poverty
* weak infrastructure
* poor governance
* unsafe settlements
* lack of awareness
* high population density

This approach shifts the focus from **hazard** → to **people and systems**.

---

# **4. *Development Approach***

Disasters and development are interconnected.

### **Key idea:**

* Bad development increases disaster risk (unplanned construction, deforestation)
* Good development reduces risk (strong infrastructure, awareness, safety laws)

This is heavily used in UN and NDMA frameworks.

---

# **5. *Comprehensive or Holistic Approach***

This is the gold-standard view.

It combines:

* physical
* social
* economic
* environmental
* institutional
* technological factors

### **Why it matters:**

Disasters are complex.
A purely structural or social view is incomplete.
Holistic view = full picture.

---

# **6. *Risk Management Approach***

This approach focuses on **reducing risk** rather than reacting to disasters.

### Key steps:

* risk identification
* risk analysis
* risk reduction
* preparedness
* response
* recovery

This aligns with the **Disaster Management Cycle**.

---

# **7. *Emergency Management Approach***

This is purely response-focused:

* evacuations
* rescue
* relief
* medical care

### Weakness:

It ignores long-term mitigation and prevention.

---

# **8. *Environmental Approach***

Disasters are seen as outcomes of environmental degradation.

Examples:

* floods due to deforestation
* landslides due to mining
* drought due to climate change

This approach is increasingly important due to global warming.

---

# **Exam-Ready 4–8 Mark Answer (Condensed)**

Approaches to understanding disasters include:

1. **Natural Event Approach** – disasters seen as natural phenomena.
2. **Physical/Engineering Approach** – focuses on structural failures and physical vulnerability.
3. **Social Vulnerability Approach** – emphasizes human and societal weaknesses.
4. **Development Approach** – links disaster risk with development quality.
5. **Comprehensive/Holistic Approach** – integrates physical, social, economic, and environmental factors.
6. **Risk Management Approach** – focuses on systematic risk reduction.
7. **Emergency Management Approach** – concentrates on rescue and relief actions.
8. **Environmental Approach** – views disasters as consequences of environmental degradation.
