# **A. Response Time of Hazards**

**Response time = the gap between the occurrence of a hazard and the onset of its damaging impact.**

In simple terms:
ðŸ‘‰ How quickly does the hazard hit?

This determines how fast authorities and people need to act.

### **1. Sudden/rapid-onset hazards (very short response time)**

You get little to no time to react.

Examples:

* Earthquakes
* Flash floods
* Lightning
* Tornadoes
* Industrial explosions
* Building collapse

**Response time: seconds to minutes**
This leads to high fatalities if preparedness is poor.

---

### **2. Slow-onset hazards (long response time)**

These develop gradually.

Examples:

* Drought
* Heatwaves
* Cold waves
* Epidemics
* Desertification

**Response time: days to months**
This allows early planning and mitigation.
