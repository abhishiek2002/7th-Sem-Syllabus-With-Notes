# **9. Types of Disasters**

*(Importance: Very High — this is a direct question source.)*

Your syllabus includes multiple types. Don’t waste time writing stories.
Write **definition + cause + impact** for each.

We’ll take them one by one.

---

# **1. Floods**

### **What it is:**

Overflow of water submerging normally dry land.

### **Causes:**

* Heavy rainfall
* River overflow
* Dam failure
* Poor drainage
* Cloudburst

### **Impact / Damage:**

* Drowning casualties
* Crop loss
* Water contamination → epidemics
* Destruction of houses and roads
* Mass displacement

Floods are **the most frequent hazard** in India.

---

# **2. Cyclones**

### **What it is:**

Intense low-pressure system with strong winds, heavy rain, and storm surge.

### **Causes:**

* Warm ocean water
* Atmospheric instability
* Pressure difference

### **Impact:**

* Destruction by wind
* Storm surge (most deadly)
* Coastal flooding
* Communication & power failure
* Large-scale evacuation needed

Bay of Bengal cyclones are the most dangerous in India.

---

# **3. Lightning**

### **What it is:**

Sudden electrical discharge between clouds or cloud-to-ground.

### **Causes:**

* Atmospheric instability
* Charge separation in clouds

### **Impact:**

* Instant death
* Forest and house fires
* Damage to electrical systems

Lightning kills **more people annually in India** than any other hazard except heatwaves.

---

# **4. Thunderstorms**

### **What it is:**

Storms with thunder, lightning, strong winds, and sometimes hail.

### **Causes:**

* Rapid upward movement of warm, moist air
* Atmospheric instability

### **Impact:**

* Property damage
* Power outages
* Lightning injuries
* Localized flooding
* Hail damage to crops

Common during pre-monsoon season.

---

# **5. Hailstorms**

### **What it is:**

Balls of ice (hail) falling from cumulonimbus clouds.

### **Impact:**

* Severe crop damage
* Vehicle and roof destruction
* Injuries to people and livestock

High agricultural damage hazard.

---

# **6. Avalanches**

### **What it is:**

Rapid slide of snow/ice down a mountain slope.

### **Causes:**

* Unstable snow layers
* Earthquakes
* Heavy snowfall
* Human activity

### **Impact:**

* Burial of settlements
* Death of mountaineers/soldiers
* Blocked roads

Frequent in Himalayas.

---

# **7. Drought**

### **What it is:**

Prolonged period of deficient rainfall.

### **Impact:**

* Crop failure
* Water scarcity
* Livestock deaths
* Migration
* Economic loss

Slow-onset but extremely destructive long-term.

---

# **8. Cold Waves / Heat Waves**

### **Heat Waves:**

Abnormally high temperatures for prolonged periods.

**Impact:** heart strokes, dehydration, death, crop stress.

### **Cold Waves:**

Very low temperatures + chilly winds.

**Impact:** hypothermia, livestock death, crop damage.

Heatwaves in India are becoming **more intense** due to climate change.

---

# **9. Epidemics**

### **What it is:**

Large-scale spread of infectious disease.

### **Impact:**

* High mortality
* Economic shutdown
* Healthcare overload

Examples: Covid-19, dengue, swine flu.

---

# **10. Pest Attacks**

### **Examples:**

Locust swarms, crop-eating insects.

### **Impact:**

* Total crop loss
* Food shortages
* Economic damage

---

# **11. Forest Fires**

### **What it is:**

Uncontrolled fire in forests.

### **Causes:**

Heat, dryness, lightning, human negligence.

### **Impact:**

* Loss of biodiversity
* Air pollution
* Soil degradation
* Property destruction

---

# **Man-made Disasters (brief)**

### **12. Industrial Disasters**

* Gas leaks (Bhopal)
* Chemical explosions
* Boiler blasts

### **13. Radiological/Nuclear Disasters**

* Reactor accidents (Fukushima, Chernobyl)
* Radiation leakage

### **14. Building Collapse**

* Poor construction
* Overload
* Earthquake-triggered

### **15. Urban/Rural Fires**

* Short circuit
* Gas leakage
* Slum fires

### **16. Road & Rail Accidents**

India has one of the highest accident rates in the world.

---

# **Exam-ready short answer (4–6 marks)**

Types of disasters include natural disasters such as floods, cyclones, lightning, thunderstorms, hailstorms, avalanches, drought, heatwaves, cold waves, epidemics, pest attacks, and forest fires. Man-made disasters include industrial accidents, chemical leaks, nuclear incidents, building collapse, fires, and transport accidents. Each type has specific causes and distinct damage potential affecting life, property, and the environment.
