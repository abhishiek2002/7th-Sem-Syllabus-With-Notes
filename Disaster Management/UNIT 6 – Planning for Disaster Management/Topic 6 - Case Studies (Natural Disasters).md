# **6. Case Study – Natural Disaster: 2013 Kedarnath Cloudburst & Floods**

*(Importance: Extremely High — asked in college/state exams regularly)*

---

# **A. What Actually Happened? (Event Summary)**

* **Dates:** 14–17 June 2013
* **Location:** Kedarnath, Rudraprayag district, Uttarakhand
* **Event:**

  * **Cloudburst** → extreme localized rainfall
  * **Glacial lake (Chorabari Lake) burst**
  * **Flash floods + landslides**

A deadly combination of meteorology + geology + human vulnerability.

Rainfall was around **375% above normal** in just a few days.

---

# **B. Scientific Causes (Hazard Explanation)**

### **1. Cloudburst (Primary Trigger)**

* Oversaturated monsoon clouds trapped by Himalayan topography
* Sudden downpour of **100+ mm rain per hour**
* Extremely localized but massively intense rainfall

Cloudbursts in Himalayas → instant flash floods.

---

### **2. Glacial Lake Outburst Flood (GLOF)**

* Chorabari Lake (glacial moraine lake) failed
* Lake water + debris rushed down at high speed
* Multiplied flood intensity

This GLOF was a major reason for Kedarnath town being wiped out.

---

### **3. Steep Himalayan Slopes**

* High gradient → water accelerates instantly
* No time for warning or escape

---

### **4. Unstable Geology**

* Young Himalayan mountains = fragile
* Rainfall + vibrations triggered massive landslides

---

# **C. Why the Damage Was So Extreme? (Vulnerability Factors)**

### **1. Unplanned Construction Near Riverbanks**

Hotels, roads, houses built **very close to Mandakini river**.
Most were illegal or structurally weak.

### **2. Tourist/ Pilgrim Rush**

* June = peak yatra season
* 1 lakh+ tourists and pilgrims present
* High population density = high casualty potential

### **3. No Strict Land-Use Control**

River floodplains encroached everywhere.

### **4. Deforestation & Road Cutting**

* Weak slopes
* More landslides

### **5. Lack of Early Warning Systems**

No weather radar coverage
No flood alarms
No glacial lake monitoring

### **6. Poor Disaster Preparedness**

* No evacuation plan
* No trained local teams
* No awareness among pilgrims

This was a **classic case of hazard × high vulnerability = disaster**.

---

# **D. Impact of the Disaster**

## **1. Human Impact**

* Estimated **5,000–6,000 deaths**
* Thousands missing
* Severe injuries, trauma

Actual numbers still unknown due to washed-away bodies.

---

## **2. Infrastructure Damage**

* Kedarnath town nearly destroyed
* Roads, bridges, power lines wiped out
* Over 4,000 villages affected
* Massive landslides cut off entire regions

---

## **3. Property & Economic Loss**

* Crops destroyed
* Tourism halted
* Livelihoods wiped out
* Estimated loss: **₹7,500+ crore**

---

## **4. Environmental Impact**

* River course changed
* Massive soil erosion
* Uprooted forests
* Permanent geomorphological changes

---

# **E. Disaster Management Actions (Response Analysis)**

### **1. Rescue Operations**

* **Indian Army**
* **ITBP**
* **NDRF**
* **Air Force helicopters**

Evacuation was extremely difficult due to altitude, terrain, and weather.

Helicopters made thousands of sorties to rescue stranded pilgrims.

---

### **2. Relief Camps**

* Food, water, medicines arranged in safe zones
* Temporary shelters built
* Medical teams deployed

---

### **3. Search & Identification**

* Dead bodies recovered for weeks
* DNA testing for identification
* Avoided epidemic outbreaks through sanitation

---

### **4. Restoration Work**

* Roads rebuilt
* Communication restored
* Reopening of Char Dham routes

---

# **F. Long-Term Measures (Rehabilitation & Policy Changes)**

### **1. Char Dham Reconstruction Plan**

* Wide roads
* Safe bridges
* Slope stabilization
* Landslide mitigation

### **2. Relocation of vulnerable villages**

### **3. Banning new construction near rivers**

### **4. Improved meteorological forecasting**

* Doppler radars installed
* Glacial lake monitoring started

### **5. Focus on sustainable tourism**

Reduce pressure during yatra season.

---

# **G. Lessons Learned (The Scoring Section)**

* Cloudbursts + unplanned development = guaranteed disaster
* Himalayan ecology must be respected
* Avoid construction on river floodplains
* Early warning systems are critical
* Need strict land-use regulations
* Disaster preparedness must include **pilgrimage management**
* Glacial lake monitoring is essential

---

# **H. Exam-Ready Short Answer (6–8 marks)**

**The 2013 Kedarnath disaster was caused by a cloudburst and the sudden breach of Chorabari Lake, resulting in flash floods and landslides. Unplanned construction along the Mandakini River, deforestation, unstable Himalayan slopes, and the presence of thousands of pilgrims increased vulnerability. Over 5,000 people died and entire settlements, roads, and bridges were destroyed. Response operations involved the Army, ITBP, NDRF, and massive helicopter evacuations. Reconstruction focused on slope stabilization, safer infrastructure, and improved early warning systems. The event highlighted the need for sustainable development, strict land-use regulations, and better preparedness in ecologically fragile zones.**
