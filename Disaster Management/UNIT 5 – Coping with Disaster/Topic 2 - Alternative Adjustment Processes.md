# **2. Alternative Adjustment Processes**

# **A. What Does This Mean? (Clear Definition)**

**Alternative adjustment processes are the different ways people, families, and communities modify their behavior, lifestyle, and living conditions to cope with disaster impacts and adapt to new realities.**

In simple terms:
üëâ They are **changes people make** to survive and continue life after a disaster.

These adjustments can be:

* temporary
* permanent
* economic
* social
* environmental

And they happen because **normal routines become impossible**.

---

# **B. Why Are Alternative Adjustment Processes Needed?**

Because disasters disrupt:

* homes
* jobs
* transport
* health
* safety
* environment
* food supply

People have no choice but to adapt.

---

# **C. Types of Alternative Adjustment Processes (Scoring Section)**

Write at least 5‚Äì6 of these for a full answer.

---

## **1. Temporary Relocation**

People shift to safer areas for days or months.

### Example:

* Flood victims staying in school shelters
* Cyclone-affected families staying in community centers

---

## **2. Permanent Relocation or Resettlement**

If the original area becomes unsafe.

### Example:

* Moving villages away from riverbanks
* Relocation after landslides or coastal erosion

Sometimes forced, sometimes voluntary.

---

## **3. Change of Livelihood / Occupation**

If the main source of income is damaged.

### Example:

* Farmers shifting to wage labor after drought
* Fishermen working in construction after cyclones
* Small shop owners changing trade due to losses

---

## **4. Modifying Housing Structure**

People rebuild homes with safer materials or design changes.

### Example:

* Using flood-resistant plinth elevation
* Reinforcing roofs after cyclones
* Earthquake-safe construction

This is a long-term adjustment.

---

## **5. Changing Consumption Patterns**

People adapt due to scarcity of resources.

### Examples:

* Reducing food consumption
* Using alternative fuel sources
* Prioritizing essential items

Not ideal, but common in disasters.

---

## **6. Social Adjustments**

Changes in social behaviour or routines.

### Example:

* Community sharing of water and food
* Joint childcare in shelters
* Relying more on extended family or neighbors

Social cohesion becomes survival strategy.

---

## **7. Psychological Adjustment**

Mental adaptation to trauma and stress.

### Examples:

* Seeking counselling
* Community prayer/meetings for emotional support
* Adjusting expectations about recovery timeline

Mental coping is as important as physical coping.

---

## **8. Migration (Short-term or long-term)**

People temporarily or permanently move to search for work or safety.

### Example:

* Post-disaster migration to cities
* Seasonal migration after crop failure

Very common after repeated disasters.

---

## **9. Adoption of New Survival Strategies**

People develop new methods to handle future risks.

### Example:

* Storing more food/water
* Keeping emergency kits
* Avoiding risky locations
* Reinforcing houses

This becomes part of long-term resilience.

---

# **D. Characteristics of Adjustment Processes**

Examiners sometimes ask this indirectly.

* They are **adaptive**, not fixed
* Mostly based on available resources
* May be **short-term or long-term**
* Can be **positive (safe)** or **negative (risky)**
* Often influenced by poverty, education, and community support

---

# **E. Example to Use in Answers**

Cyclone-affected coastal family:

* Moves temporarily to cyclone shelter
* Shifts livelihood from fishing to daily wage labor
* Repairs house with stronger roof
* Reduces expenses
* Participates in community rebuilding groups

Realistic examples = higher score.
