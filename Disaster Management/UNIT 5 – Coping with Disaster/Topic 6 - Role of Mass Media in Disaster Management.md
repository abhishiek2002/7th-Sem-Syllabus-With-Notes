# **6. Role of Mass Media in Disaster Management**

*(Importance: Medium — frequently asked for 4–6 marks)*

Mass media includes:

* TV
* Radio
* Newspapers
* Social media
* Internet news
* Mobile alerts
* Community radio

Media plays a critical role in ALL stages of disaster management.

---

# **A. Role Before a Disaster (Preparedness Stage)**

## **1. Early Warning Dissemination**

Media spreads warnings quickly through:

* TV breaking news
* Radio bulletins
* Social media alerts
* Mobile notifications
* Government announcements

Fast warnings = fewer deaths.

---

## **2. Public Awareness & Education**

Media educates people on:

* evacuation routes
* do’s and don’ts during disasters
* first aid steps
* helpline numbers
* safe shelter information

Awareness reduces panic and mistakes.

---

## **3. Risk Communication**

Mass media informs communities about:

* upcoming cyclones
* heatwave alerts
* flood forecasts
* drought warnings
* epidemic advisories

Good risk communication builds preparedness.

---

# **B. Role During a Disaster (Response Stage)**

## **1. Real-Time Information Sharing**

Media provides updates on:

* affected areas
* road blockages
* weather conditions
* rising water levels
* fire spread direction

This helps authorities and the public take correct actions.

---

## **2. Guiding People Toward Safety**

Media broadcasts:

* shelter locations
* medical camp details
* emergency helpline numbers
* relief distribution points

This saves lives.

---

## **3. Coordinating Rescue Efforts**

Media alerts rescue teams by showing:

* trapped individuals
* isolated villages
* collapsed buildings
* urgent medical needs

Sometimes rescue teams act based on media broadcasts.

---

## **4. Preventing Rumors & Panic**

Verified news prevents:

* fake alerts
* panic migration
* misinformation
* social chaos

Media must act responsibly here.

---

# **C. Role After a Disaster (Recovery Stage)**

## **1. Monitoring Relief Distribution**

Media exposes:

* delays
* corruption
* mismanagement
* unfair distribution

This forces authorities to act faster.

---

## **2. Mobilizing Public Support**

Media campaigns collect:

* donations
* blood
* medicines
* food
* volunteers

Public support helps faster recovery.

---

## **3. Highlighting Rehabilitation Needs**

Media brings attention to:

* areas still suffering
* families needing support
* infrastructure still damaged

This ensures long-term help is provided.

---

## **4. Psychological Support**

Stories of survival and hope give:

* confidence
* emotional healing
* motivation for affected communities

Positive media coverage reduces trauma.

---

# **D. Advantages of Mass Media in Disaster Management**

* Fast communication
* Wide reach (urban + rural)
* Reliable platform for official warnings
* Reduces time for evacuation
* Helps coordinate multiple agencies
* Improves transparency

---

# **E. Challenges / Limitations**

If you write these, you score higher.

* Spread of fake news
* Panic due to exaggerated reporting
* Misinterpretation of warnings
* Biased or sensational coverage
* Poor reach in remote areas

Acknowledging problems shows deeper understanding.

---

# **F. Exam-Ready Short Answer (4–6 marks)**

**Mass media plays a crucial role in disaster management by disseminating early warnings, building public awareness, and educating communities before disasters. During disasters, it provides real-time information, guides people to shelters, supports rescue coordination, and prevents rumors. After disasters, mass media monitors relief distribution, mobilizes donations, highlights rehabilitation needs, and offers psychological support. Media enhances transparency, speeds up response, and strengthens overall disaster communication.**
