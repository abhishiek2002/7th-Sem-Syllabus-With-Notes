# **A. Safety Norms**

**Safety norms are the rules, standards, and practices that ensure protection of life, property, and environment during disasters and emergencies.**

These norms apply at home, workplace, schools, industries, and public spaces.

---

# **A1. Key Safety Norms (Write these for full marks)**

## **1. Structural Safety Norms**

* Earthquake-resistant construction
* Fire-safe electrical wiring
* Proper ventilation
* Safe storage of hazardous materials
* Regular building inspections

---

## **2. Fire Safety Norms**

* Fire extinguishers in accessible locations
* Smoke detectors & alarm systems
* No blocked exits
* Fire-resistant materials
* Regular fire drills

---

## **3. Industrial Safety Norms**

* Use of PPE (helmets, gloves, masks)
* SOP for handling chemicals
* Gas leak detectors
* Safety valves
* Pressure checks for boilers

---

## **4. Household Safety Norms**

* Turn off electricity/gas during disasters
* Avoid using elevators during earthquakes
* Secure heavy furniture
* Keep emergency numbers handy
* Safe cooking practices to avoid fires

---

## **5. Road Safety Norms**

* Follow traffic rules
* Use helmets and seatbelts
* Avoid driving during heavy rain/flood
* No overloading of vehicles

---

## **6. School Safety Norms**

* Earthquake and fire drills
* Safe school building design
* Emergency exits marked clearly
* Training teachers and students

---

## **7. Electrical Safety Norms**

* Avoid overloaded sockets
* Proper grounding
* Regular wiring checks
* Avoid touching electrical items during flooding

---

# **A2. Purpose of Safety Norms**

Write this to score extra:

* Prevent accidents
* Reduce casualties
* Ensure quick evacuation
* Maintain order during emergencies
* Create a culture of safety

---

# **B. Survival Kits**

**A survival kit (Emergency Go-Bag) is a collection of essential items required for survival during the first 24–72 hours of a disaster.**

Disasters like earthquakes, cyclones, floods, and fires require families to evacuate quickly.
A survival kit increases the chance of **staying alive and staying safe** until help arrives.

---

# **B1. Essential Items in a Survival Kit**

## **1. Water & Food**

* Bottled water (3 liters per person per day)
* Dry food (biscuits, energy bars, ready-to-eat food)

---

## **2. First Aid Supplies**

* Bandages
* Antiseptic
* Painkillers
* ORS packets
* Personal medicines
* Gloves

---

## **3. Communication & Light**

* Battery-powered/hand-crank radio
* Torch/flashlight
* Extra batteries
* Whistle (for signaling)

---

## **4. Important Documents (in waterproof pouch)**

* ID proof
* ATM cards
* Insurance papers
* Medical records
* Photographs
* Cash

---

## **5. Clothing & Protection**

* Extra clothes
* Raincoat
* Sturdy shoes
* Warm clothing
* Masks

---

## **6. Tools & Utility Items**

* Multipurpose knife
* Rope
* Matchbox/lighter
* Duct tape
* Small toolkit
* Plastic sheets

---

## **7. Hygiene Items**

* Soap
* Sanitizer
* Toothpaste
* Sanitary pads
* Baby items (if required)

---

## **8. Special Needs Items**

For children, elderly, disabled people:

* diapers
* wheelchairs
* prescription medicines
* spectacles
* hearing aids

---

# **B2. Why Survival Kits Are Important**

* Helps during sudden evacuation
* Reduces panic
* Ensures basic survival until help arrives
* Supports self-sufficiency
* Reduces dependency on immediate relief

This shows understanding — not just listing items.

---

# **C. Exam-Ready Short Answer (4–5 marks)**

**Safety norms are the rules and practices designed to prevent accidents and protect people during disasters, including structural safety measures, fire safety norms, industrial safety guidelines, household and school safety standards, and electrical safety practices. A survival kit is an emergency bag containing essential items such as food, water, first aid, communication tools, important documents, clothing, hygiene items, and special-needs supplies. Survival kits help families survive the first 24–72 hours of a disaster and ensure quick evacuation and self-sufficiency.**
