# **A. What Are Coping Strategies? (Precise Definition)**

**Coping strategies are the methods, actions, and adjustments adopted by individuals, families, and communities to survive, manage, and recover from disaster impacts using available resources.**

In simple words:
üëâ How people **manage** during and after a disaster, especially when external help is limited.

Coping can be:

* Immediate
* Short-term
* Long-term

And it can be **positive** or **negative**.

---

# **B. Types of Coping Strategies (Scoring Section)**

Examiners expect categories + examples.

---

## **1. Preventive Coping Strategies (Before Disaster)**

Actions taken to reduce impact.

### Examples:

* Storing food and water before a cyclone
* Strengthening house structure
* Keeping emergency kits
* Understanding warning signals
* Purchasing insurance
* Protecting important documents

Preventive coping reduces initial shock.

---

## **2. Emergency Coping Strategies (During Disaster)**

Immediate actions to survive.

### Examples:

* Evacuating to shelters
* Using emergency supplies
* Saving important belongings
* Taking refuge on higher ground during floods
* First aid to injured family members
* Turning off electricity/gas during earthquakes

These strategies reduce casualties.

---

## **3. Short-Term Coping Strategies (Immediately After Disaster)**

Actions during early recovery.

### Examples:

* Living in temporary shelters
* Sharing resources within community
* Repairing damaged houses temporarily
* Using savings to buy essentials
* Seeking assistance from relatives/Friends

Short-term coping focuses on stabilizing life.

---

## **4. Long-Term Coping Strategies (Months/Years After Disaster)**

Sustaining recovery over time.

### Examples:

* Rebuilding houses
* Switching to safer livelihoods
* Taking loans or insurance claims
* Participating in community rebuilding
* Adopting safer construction practices
* Migrating temporarily for work

Long-term coping determines resilience.

---

## **5. Social Coping Strategies**

Using social networks and community bonds.

### Examples:

* Mutual aid groups
* Sharing food, tools, and labour
* Community shelters
* Local volunteers‚Äô support
* Panchayat/SHG assistance

Communities with strong social bonds recover faster.

---

## **6. Psychological Coping Strategies**

Managing stress and trauma.

### Examples:

* Counselling
* Emotional support from family
* Stress-relief activities
* Community meetings
* Religious/community rituals for emotional stability

Psychological coping prevents long-term mental damage.

---

## **7. Negative Coping Strategies (Must Mention at least one)**

Sometimes people use harmful methods.

### Examples:

* Taking high-interest loans
* Selling productive assets (cattle, tools, land)
* Reducing food intake
* Child labour
* Risky migration

These reduce future resilience.
Mentioning negative coping shows deeper understanding ‚Äî good for long answers.

# **E. Exam-Ready Short Answer (4‚Äì6 marks)**

**Coping strategies are the actions adopted by individuals and communities to survive and recover from disasters using available resources. They include preventive strategies such as storing essentials and strengthening homes; emergency strategies such as evacuation and first aid; short-term coping through temporary shelters and mutual support; and long-term strategies like rebuilding and livelihood restoration. Coping may be social, economic, psychological, and can include both positive and negative behaviors. Effective coping reduces vulnerability and speeds up recovery.**
