# **6. Disaster Management Cycle**

*(Importance: Very High — ALWAYS asked: short note, diagram, or full long answer.)*

Most students write random steps.
You need the **official, globally accepted cycle** with proper explanation.

The Disaster Management Cycle has **4 main stages**, often divided into **8 sub-stages**.
Write whichever your exam demands.

---

# ⭐ The 4-Stage Model (Most Scoring & Common)

1. **Mitigation**
2. **Preparedness**
3. **Response**
4. **Recovery**

These four stages form a continuous cycle.

Let’s explain each with points that actually score.

---

# **1. Mitigation (Pre-Disaster, Long-Term)**

**Goal:** Reduce the impact of future disasters.

Mitigation = “Make sure the disaster causes minimum damage.”

### Activities:

* Building earthquake-resistant structures
* Flood embankments
* Cyclone shelters
* Land-use planning
* Dams & drainage improvement
* Enforcing building codes
* Environmental conservation

Mitigation is **structural + non-structural**.

---

# **2. Preparedness (Pre-Disaster, Short-Term)**

**Goal:** Ensure systems and people are ready.

Preparedness = “Get ready before the disaster strikes.”

### Activities:

* Early warning systems
* Emergency communication networks
* Mock drills
* Training of community & officials
* Evacuation planning
* Stockpiling essential supplies
* Creating SOPs (Standard Operating Procedures)

Preparedness reduces panic and confusion.

---

# **3. Response (During Disaster / Immediate Post-Disaster)**

**Goal:** Save lives and reduce immediate suffering.

Response = “Act quickly when disaster hits.”

### Activities:

* Search and rescue
* Medical aid
* Evacuation
* Firefighting
* Emergency shelters
* Rapid damage assessment
* Law and order maintenance
* Distribution of relief materials

Response decides how many lives are saved.

---

# **4. Recovery (Post-Disaster, Long-Term)**

**Goal:** Restore normal life and rebuild better.

Recovery = “Bring life back to normal — and stronger.”

### Phases:

#### **Short-Term Recovery**

* Temporary shelters
* Restoration of water, electricity, transport
* Clearing debris

#### **Long-Term Recovery**

* Reconstruction of houses, roads, schools
* Economic rehabilitation
* Psychological support
* Livelihood restoration
* Insurance and compensation

Recovery can last months or years.

# **Exam-Ready Short Answer (4–6 marks)**

**The Disaster Management Cycle refers to the continuous process of planning and managing disasters through mitigation, preparedness, response, and recovery. Mitigation and preparedness occur before disasters to reduce risk. Response takes place during disasters to save lives, while recovery focuses on rehabilitation and reconstruction after the disaster. The cycle is continuous and ensures systematic and effective disaster management.**
