# **A. What is Capacity Building? (Precise Definition)**

**Capacity building is the process of developing, strengthening, and improving the abilities, skills, resources, institutions, and systems needed to effectively prevent, prepare for, respond to, and recover from disasters.**

* community strength
* government systems

1. Reduces vulnerability

# **C. Elements of Capacity Building (Very Scoring)**

These are the core components examiners expect.

---

## **1. Human Resource Development**

Training and educating:

* officials
* volunteers
* community members
* medical teams
* school and college students

Includes mock drills, awareness campaigns, first-aid training.

---

## **2. Infrastructure Development**

Strengthening physical systems:

* emergency operation centers
* flood shelters
* cyclone shelters
* seismic-resistant buildings
* communication towers
* fire stations
* warehouses for relief materials

Better infrastructure = better capacity.

---

## **3. Institutional Development**

Strengthening organizations responsible for disaster management:

* NDMA, SDMA, DDMA
* NDRF & SDRF
* local self-government bodies
* NGOs & community groups

Includes laws, policies, SOPs, coordination systems.

---

## **4. Community-Based Capacity Building**

Building capacity at the **local** level:

* training youth volunteers
* forming disaster management committees
* local evacuation planning
* community drills
* awareness on early warning signs

Communities are first responders ‚Äî not the government.

---

## **5. Technology & Equipment Capacity**

Developing and maintaining necessary tools:

* GIS and hazard maps
* early warning systems
* satellite communication
* rescue equipment
* emergency transport vehicles

Technology is essential for speed & accuracy.

---

## **6. Financial Capacity**

Funds are critical for disaster handling:

* disaster relief funds (NDRF, SDRF)
* insurance schemes
* dedicated budgets
* emergency funds at district/block level

Without money, no action is possible.
