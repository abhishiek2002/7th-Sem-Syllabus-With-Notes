# **5. Counter-Disaster Resources & Their Utility**

*(Importance: Medium–High — common 4–6 mark question)*

**Counter-disaster resources** are all the tools, people, equipment, institutions, and materials used to **prepare for, respond to, and recover from disasters.**

Think of them as **everything needed to fight a disaster effectively.**

We’ll categorize them clearly.

---

# **A. Human Resources (Most important)**

These are trained people involved in disaster management.

### **Examples:**

* NDRF (National Disaster Response Force)
* SDRF (State Disaster Response Force)
* Police
* Fire services
* Medical teams
* Army, Navy, Air Force
* Local volunteers
* NGOs (Red Cross, SEEDS, CARE India)
* Community emergency response teams

### **Utility:**

* Search and rescue
* Evacuation
* First aid
* Firefighting
* Crowd management
* Medical care
* Camp management

Human resources are the backbone of disaster response.

---

# **B. Physical / Material Resources**

These are tangible items needed during disasters.

### **Examples:**

* Boats, helicopters, vehicles
* Ambulances
* Tents, tarpaulin sheets
* Blankets, clothing
* Water purification units
* Generators
* Rescue tools (cutters, ropes, inflatable boats)
* Medicines and medical equipment
* Communication devices (satellite phones, radios)

### **Utility:**

* Evacuation
* Relief camp setup
* Power backup
* Medical treatment
* Communication during network failure
* Food and water distribution

Material resources decide how fast relief can reach people.

---

# **C. Infrastructure Resources**

These are facilities essential for disaster management.

### **Examples:**

* Cyclone shelters
* Flood shelters
* Emergency Operation Centers (EOC)
* Hospitals
* Warehouses for relief materials
* Fire stations
* Communication towers
* Control rooms

### **Utility:**

* Safe shelter during disaster
* Medical care
* Storage and distribution of relief
* Coordinating emergency response

Good infrastructure = lower death toll.

---

# **D. Technological Resources**

Technology drastically improves speed & accuracy.

### **Examples:**

* GIS & hazard maps
* Remote sensing
* Weather radars
* Flood forecasting models
* Tsunami warning systems
* Drones for damage assessment
* Communication networks
* Disaster management software

### **Utility:**

* Early warning
* Accurate forecasting
* Real-time monitoring
* Quick damage assessment
* Better planning

Technology = fewer surprises.

---

# **E. Financial Resources**

Money available for disaster management.

### **Examples:**

* NDRF & SDRF funds
* State disaster mitigation funds
* District emergency budgets
* Insurance schemes
* Corporate CSR funds

### **Utility:**

* Buying relief materials
* Paying for reconstruction
* Funding rescue operations
* Supporting affected families
* Rebuilding infrastructure

Without funds, no action is possible.

---

# **F. Institutional Resources**

Institutions that manage, coordinate, and support disaster operations.

### **Examples:**

* NDMA
* SDMA
* DDMA
* IMD (meteorology)
* CWC (flood forecasting)
* ISRO (satellite data)
* Forest, Police, Health departments
* NGOs & international agencies

### **Utility:**

* Policy & planning
* Coordination
* Warning dissemination
* Mobilizing resources
* Long-term recovery support

Institutions shape the entire disaster management system.

---

# **G. Community Resources**

Local-level capability is extremely powerful.

### **Examples:**

* Community volunteers
* Local knowledge of terrain
* Traditional coping strategies
* Social networks
* SHGs, panchayats

### **Utility:**

* Fast response before external help arrives
* Locating vulnerable people
* Organizing shelters & food
* Supporting recovery

Communities are ALWAYS the first responders.

---

# **H. Exam-Ready Short Answer (4–6 marks)**

**Counter-disaster resources include human resources such as NDRF, SDRF, police, volunteers; physical resources like boats, tents, medicines, rescue tools; infrastructure such as shelters, hospitals, EOCs; technological tools like GIS, early warning systems, and forecasting models; financial resources including disaster relief funds; and institutional resources such as NDMA, SDMA, and DDMA. These resources are essential for effective preparedness, evacuation, search and rescue, relief distribution, coordination, and long-term recovery, thereby reducing disaster impact significantly.**
