# **A. What is Capacity Assessment? (Precise Definition)**

**Capacity assessment is the process of identifying and evaluating the strengths, resources, skills, institutions, and systems available in a community to effectively prepare for, respond to, and recover from disasters.**

In simple words:

It is the opposite lens of vulnerability assessment.

# **C. Types of Capacities (Very important)**

Capacity is not just equipment; it includes people, institutions, skills, and systems.

Here are the key categories:

---

## **1. Physical Capacity**

Facilities and equipment:

* Hospitals
* Ambulances
* Fire stations
* Cyclone shelters
* Boats for flood rescue
* Storage warehouses
* Communication tools
* Rescue tools (ropes, cutters, PPE kits)

**Physical capacity = things you can use physically.**

---

## **2. Human Capacity**

People and their skills:

* trained volunteers
* medical teams
* NDRF/SDRF personnel
* engineers & technicians
* teachers, leaders, community groups

Includes knowledge, training, experience.

---

## **3. Social Capacity**

Community bonds & networks:

* mutual aid groups
* local committees
* social support systems
* strong communication between people

Communities with unity recover faster.

---

## **4. Institutional Capacity**

Strength of systems and administration:

* NDMA, SDMA, DDMA
* government departments
* local government bodies
* NGOs
* emergency operation centers
* early warning systems
* policies, laws, SOPs

Strong institutions = stronger disaster resilience.

---

## **5. Economic Capacity**

Financial strength:

* government disaster funds (NDRF/SDRF)
* personal savings
* insurance coverage
* local business stability
* access to credit

Richer communities rebuild faster.

---

## **6. Environmental Capacity**

Healthy natural systems that reduce hazard impact:

* mangroves (cyclone protection)
* wetlands (flood control)
* forests (landslide prevention)

Environment is a natural protector.

---

# **D. Steps of Capacity Assessment (Use this for long answers)**

This is where you score extra.

### **1. Identify hazard-specific needs**

What type of disaster the region faces?

### **2. List available resources**

Rescue teams, shelters, hospitals, volunteers, equipment.

### **3. Evaluate effectiveness**

Are resources modern? Enough? Accessible?

### **4. Identify gaps**

Where is training needed? What equipment is missing?

### **5. Recommend improvements**

Training, budgeting, better coordination, new infrastructure.
